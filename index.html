Government and its inefficiencies

Have you ever wondered why governments are inefficient? It's due to a fundamental misalignment between reward and productivity...

Have you ever wondered why governments are inefficient? It's due to a fundamental misalignment between reward and productivity. In the free market, an individual is directly rewarded based on the amount of value they produce. This is because both parties must mutually agree to an exchange. Hence, an exchange will only occur if both parties deem what will be received as more valuable than what will be given up. If someone creates something that others subjectively deem valuable, individuals will be willing to give up some of their resources, typically in the form of money, in return for the product or service being offered. Therefore, consumer demand directly rewards the producers based on the value of their production. In other words, on the free market, if someone wants to obtain resources, the best way he can do so is by providing value to his fellow men. Yet, there's a stark difference between the mutually beneficial exchange of voluntary cooperation and government coercion. In the government, both parties don't have to agree to an exchange. This is evident by the government's use of force, in the form of taxation, to get individuals' resources. Because the government doesn't provide a product or service that competes on the free market but rather has a monopoly in its various industries with production funded by taxes, there is no incentive for the government to be efficient. Bureaucrats get paid from forcibly seized taxpayer money, regardless of whether or not the product or service provided was deemed valuable. Hence, in contrast to the free market, the government is rewarded based on the amount of resources they can forcibly take from others, not through value production. I hope this can shed light on the inevitable inefficiencies concomitant with government.

To understand why I feel stupid, let’s start from the beginning of my day. It’s 7am, and I wake up to an alarm clock whose mechanism of producing sound I don’t understand. I get up and feel the AC blowing across my body. Refreshing. Yet, how does it work? I’m rather oblivious. I turn on the sink to wash my face. Water flows. Hot, cold, and filtered. Delivered right to my tap. Yet, the logistics behind the water filtration and distribution process leave me perplexed. Time for some breakfast. I open the fridge. Again, I’m amazed, yet simultaneously unaware of the mechanisms behind something I use every day. Oats with milk. Yum. Nevertheless, I’m unsure how the oats are planted, grown, harvested, and processed. Dairy production, even more oblivious. Time to brush my teeth. Synthetic plastics and chemicals from a tube are used to keep them shiny and white. With regard to their way of results, again I’m ignorant. I ride my bike to class. An aluminum frame, rubber tires, pressurized air, and hydraulic disk brakes. All things I’m currently unable to make myself. Classes go by. Time passes through a 4th dimension. How is time measure? Why does it change? Unsure. Time for the gym. I lift weights in a battle against gravity. I’m attempting to release hormones in my brain that may leave me feeling accomplished. Two more concepts I don’t understand. Now that I’ve destroyed parts of my body, I will eat protein. How my body will use the amino acids to repair my muscle’s tears, I don’t know. I open my laptop and connect to the internet. A distributed network with data being sent via cables and the air. How? I’m unaware. I decide to write an article. My laptop converts presses of a button and slides of a finger into data for my computer to read, baffles me. I use a website called Substack to write my first article. How does this website work? Why I’m writing this article? What is the purpose of my life? I don’t know.
